# Transportation Systems and Mental Health

This project analyzes the relationship between commuting behavior, transportation dependence, and mental health outcomes across U.S. states and counties.

## Methods
- OLS Regression
- Random Forest
- XGBoost
- PCA
- K-Means Clustering

## Data Sources
- CDC BRFSS
- CDC PLACES
- American Community Survey (ACS)

## Key Findings
- Drive-alone commute share is one of the strongest predictors of mental distress
- Counties with high automobile dependence exhibit systematically worse mental health outcomes
- Transportation structure appears more important than urban form alone
